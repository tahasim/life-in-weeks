Memento Mori: A Living Practice

What is Memento Mori?

An ancient Stoic practice of meditating on your own mortality—not to be morbid, but to gain clarity. Recognizing that life is finite is the most powerful catalyst for living intentionally.

Why This Matters

When you truly internalize that your time is limited, several things shift:

  • Trivial pursuits lose their power. The things consuming your time suddenly feel different.
  • Your values become clear. What do you actually want? What matters when time is scarce?
  • Fear loses its grip. Many fears are revealed as unimportant compared to the fact of mortality.
  • Procrastination dissolves. When did you plan to start? Why not now?

The Exercise

Below is a visual representation of one year of your life—52 weeks. As you use the journaling space, ask yourself:

  • If I had only one year left— what changes immediately in my choices?
  • What am I waiting for? Why delay what matters?
  • What would I regret not doing? Now, mark it in your calendar.
  • What fears or obstacles are revealed as small in comparison? Write them down.
